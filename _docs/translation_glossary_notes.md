## Revision Notes

Add notes here when a later chapter forces a change to an earlier translation choice.

- For front matter and prefaces, use "Volume" for `Band` and "Preface" for `Vorrede`.
- Use "principle of sufficient reason" for `Satz vom Grunde` beginning in the editor's notes.
- In chapter 0003, `Erkenntniß` was translated as "cognition" when referring to the field of what exists for knowing; continue to prefer "cognition" in technical epistemological contexts.
- In chapter 0004, `Erkenntniß` continues as "cognition" in technical contexts, especially "object of cognition" and "`a priori` certain cognition."
- In chapter 0005, use "grounding" for `Begründung` when Schopenhauer discusses the law of the grounding of judgments.
- In chapter 0006, use "understanding" for `Verstand`, since Schopenhauer defines it as the faculty of causal cognition that makes intuition possible.
- In chapter 0006, use "actuality" for `Wirklichkeit` when Schopenhauer explicitly contrasts it with `Realität`.
- In chapter 0007, keep the distinction between empirical reality and transcendental ideality: the world is empirically real as causally connected representation, but transcendentally ideal because objecthood is conditioned by the subject.
- In chapter 0011, translate `Sphäre` as "sphere" in the logical diagrams/relations, and `Umfang` as "extension" when paired with it.
- In chapter 0007, `Wirklichkeit` is translated as "actuality" in the dream/waking discussion when contrasted with dream or phantasm.
- In chapter 0008, use "understanding" consistently for `Verstand`; Schopenhauer extends it to all animals and distinguishes it sharply from human reason.
- In chapter 0008, use "semblance" for `Schein` when it is opposed to reality as deception of the understanding; use "error" for `Irrthum` as deception of reason.
- In chapter 0009, preserve Schopenhauer's methodological starting point: neither subject nor object, but representation as the first fact of consciousness.
- In chapter 0009, translate `Vernunft-Anschauung` as "rational intuition" and keep the polemical tone around identity philosophy.
- In chapter 0009, keep "no object without subject" as the concise formula against materialism.
- In chapter 0010, reason's single function is "formation of the concept"; preserve this formulation.
- In chapter 0010, keep the contrast: understanding has immediate cognition of causality, reason forms concepts.
- From chapter 0003 onward, translations should improve English paragraphing rather than mirror the source paragraph structure one-to-one.
